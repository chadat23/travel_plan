Park Location Proposal Notes:
  1. Go to the All Locations page to make sure that the location doesn't already exist, perhaps with a different from expected name.
  2. Use a site such as latlong.net to get the latitude and longitude of the proposed location.
    • For landmarks that cover an appreciable area such as meadows, basins, and lakes, use the geographic center.
    • For landmarks that are points such as peaks, trail heads, and intersections, be as accurate as possible.
    • For landmarks that cover sprawling or imprecise areas of the park such as Hetch Hetchy, Tuolumne Meadows, or the Merced River, consider a more precise name and location.
  3. Use at least 6 decimal places when entering locations.
  4. Add a note justifying why the location should be added. Please note that not all locations in the park can be added so a proposed location may be rejected if it isn't deemed sufficiently important or relevant.
